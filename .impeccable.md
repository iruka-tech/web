## Design Context

### Users
Megabat is used by DeFi operators, protocol analysts, and agent builders who need a serious monitoring surface for defining, reviewing, and acting on blockchain signals. They use it as an operating console, not as a marketing-first product. The signed-in app should feel calm, exact, and trustworthy while still carrying a distinctive Megabat identity.

### Brand Personality
Megabat should feel watchful, surgical, and nocturnal. The interface should evoke vigilance, precision, and readiness instead of hype. The emotional target is confidence under pressure: the product is always listening, filtering, and surfacing only what matters.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark operational theme inspired by echolocation, cave mineral tones, and ember-lit instrumentation. The visual system should be restrained in the product workspace and more atmospheric on marketing surfaces. Accent color should feel like warmed copper or ember, not neon crypto orange. Avoid generic SaaS gradients, gamer glow, glassmorphism, playful rounded shapes, and purple-blue "AI" palettes.

### Design Principles
- Design the system first: tokens, surfaces, type, spacing, and interaction states should drive page work.
- Keep workspace pages sparse and operational: current state, one clear next action, low visual noise.
- Make Megabat memorable through atmosphere and structure, not gimmicks: echo rings, scan lines, and instrument-like framing should be subtle and purposeful.
- Use calm hierarchy over loud emphasis: regular weight text, strong spacing, precise borders, and contrast should carry the interface.
- Remove one-off styling and repeated page-local patterns in favor of reusable primitives and shared utility classes.
